How it Works

  • Attack on Availability

    Itâ€™s a type of Denial of Service attack that disrupts communication between a user a wireless access point. It exploits management frames that are normally used to maintain the link between the two devices. By spoofing either the AP or the client a hacker can constantly send these frames disconnecting the user and preventing them from reconnecting.

  • ESP8266 Microcontroller

    A cheap and easily programmable microcontroller with a full TCP/IP stack. A Linux machine with a wireless NIC and aircrack-ng can be used as substitute. You specify either a client or a access point and the device begins to spoof the device and constantly sends deauthentication frames disconnecting the client and forcing them to reconnect.

  • Users Perspective

    Their device constantly disconnected. They can continue to try to reauthticate and even if they do reconnect they will eventually get kicked back off the network as long as the attacker is sending the frames.

  • Countermeasure 802.11w

    Amendment to the IEEE 802.11 standard to protect these management frames. Sometimes labled as Protected Management Frames (PMF). Not all devices support 802.11w. If an access point is setup to require the standard devices that do not support cannot associate the WLAN.
